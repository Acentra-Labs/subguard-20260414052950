It automates the verification of Workers' Compensation and General Liability certificates, sends automated renewal requests to insurance agents via one-click email links, and provides real-time compliance dashboards — replacing the spreadsheets, phone calls, and manual certificate chasing that currently consume hours per payment cycle.\n\n## Target Users\n- **Primary:** Construction compliance consultants (Dawn and future consultants she recruits) who manage insurance verification for multiple GC clients\n- **Secondary:** General Contractors (small to mid-size, 10-50 subcontractors per job) who need quick visibility into sub compliance before each payment draw\n- **Tertiary:** Insurance Agents/Brokers who receive automated certificate requests and respond via secure one-click links (no account required)\n- **Quaternary:** Subcontractors who may optionally upload their own certificates (post-MVP)\n\n## Core Problem\nGeneral contractors must verify that every subcontractor carries current Workers' Comp and General Liability insurance before each payment — otherwise the GC faces audit liability and personal injury claims (under Idaho Code §72-216, GCs become the statutory employer of uninsured subs' workers). Currently, a consultant like Dawn spends hours manually calling 15-20 different insurance agencies per job, per draw cycle. Certificates can say they're valid for a year, but subcontractors cancel policies between jobs — especially sole proprietors exempt from WC under §72-212 who drop coverage to save money. Existing solutions (Procore, HCSS, Avetta, myCOI) are enterprise-focused, start at $200+/month with 200+ COI minimums, and are widely criticized as overly complex. The market has a clear gap for a simple, affordable tool targeting small GCs and independent consultants.\n\n## Platform Recommendation\n**Web App (Responsive)** — A responsive web application accessible on desktop and mobile browsers.
